Icosahedral Symmetry Axes

The icosahedron has 3 types of rotational symmetry axes, each passing through a different geometric feature. The holes on a Zometool ball sit exactly where these axes pierce the surface.

Axis Types (click to toggle)

5-Fold Axes (Pentagonal Holes)
Through opposite vertices of the icosahedron
12 vertices ÷ 2 = 6 axes → 12 holes
3-Fold Axes (Triangular Holes)
Through opposite face centers of the icosahedron
20 faces ÷ 2 = 10 axes → 20 holes
2-Fold Axes (Rectangular Holes)
Through opposite edge midpoints of the icosahedron
30 edges ÷ 2 = 15 axes → 30 holes

Understanding Rotation Order

The rotation order tells you how many times you can rotate around an axis before returning to the start:

5-fold: 360° / 5 = 72° per step (5 rotations to complete)
3-fold: 360° / 3 = 120° per step (3 rotations)
2-fold: 360° / 2 = 180° per step (2 rotations)

The hole shape on the Zometool ball matches this order — a pentagonal hole has 5-fold symmetry, triangular has 3-fold, rectangular has 2-fold.

Group Theory

The full icosahedral rotation group has 60 elements:

1 identity
+ 6×4 = 24 rotations around 5-fold axes
+ 10×2 = 20 rotations around 3-fold axes
+ 15×1 = 15 rotations around 2-fold axes
= 60 total

This is the group I (or A5), the largest finite rotation group in 3D.
